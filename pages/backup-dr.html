Backup & Disaster Recovery

Business continuity planning, disaster recovery strategies, RTO/RPO definitions, and backup testing procedures.

7.1 Key Concepts

RTO vs RPO

RECOVERY OBJECTIVES TIMELINE
    LAST BACKUP              DISASTER                    RECOVERY
                                                          
                                                          

                                                                     TIME
                                   
                 RPO           
          (Data Loss Window)             RTO              
                                  (Downtime Window)       


  RPO (Recovery Point Objective)                                             
  • Maximum acceptable data loss measured in time                            
  • Determines backup frequency                                              
  • Example: RPO of 1 hour = hourly backups minimum                          

  RTO (Recovery Time Objective)                                              
  • Maximum acceptable downtime                                              
  • Determines recovery infrastructure needed                                
  • Example: RTO of 4 hours = must restore within 4 hours

Interview Questions

7.2 Interview Questions

Q1How would you design a backup strategy for IBU?
Sample Answer

"I would implement a tiered backup strategy:

Tier 1 - Critical Systems (Student Portal, Finance):

  • Continuous replication or 15-minute snapshots
  • Local backup + Azure cloud backup
  • RTO: 2 hours, RPO: 15 minutes

Tier 2 - Important Systems (File Servers, Databases):

  • Hourly incremental, daily full backups
  • 30-day local retention, 1-year cloud archive
  • RTO: 4 hours, RPO: 1 hour

Tier 3 - Standard Systems (Workstations, Archives):

  • Daily backups, 7-day retention
  • Primarily rely on OneDrive/SharePoint for user data

Microsoft 365: Leverage native retention policies plus third-party backup (Veeam/Commvault) for long-term retention and granular recovery."

Q2How do you test disaster recovery plans?
Sample Answer

"DR testing should be regular and progressively more realistic:

  • Monthly: Backup verification - confirm backups complete, test file-level restore
  • Quarterly: Tabletop exercise - walk through scenarios with stakeholders
  • Bi-annually: Partial failover - restore critical systems to DR environment
  • Annually: Full DR test - complete failover during maintenance window

Document results, measure against RTO/RPO, and update plans based on findings."

Q3What's the difference between BCP and DRP?
Sample Answer

BCP (Business Continuity Plan):

  • Broader scope - how the entire organization continues operations
  • Includes people, processes, facilities, not just IT
  • Example: If campus is inaccessible, how do classes continue?

DRP (Disaster Recovery Plan):

  • IT-focused - restoring technology systems
  • Technical procedures for system recovery
  • Example: Steps to restore servers after hardware failure

DRP is a subset of BCP. For IBU, BCP would cover switching to remote learning while DRP covers restoring the LMS server."
